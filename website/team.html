Team Management

At the beginning of the project we decided to create sub groups to work on the main key sections of the system, these included: software, hardware, vision and safety/testing teams. We thought this would be the most optimal way to distribute the work between the group. Each group had a team leader who was in charge of making sure the sub group stayed on track and communicating with others outside the sub group. To make sure the sub teams stayed in contact with each other, we set a meeting every Monday at 11am via Microsoft Teams. This way we could update each other on the advancements and setbacks of the system, we also used Teams for regular sub team meetings.

Another means of communication we used was the messenger app Slack, this allowed us to create channels for the full team, each team sub team and different features which required communication (such as report writing and creating the videos).

As the semester went on we found the need for a few extra sub teams: reports and videos, this meant that we had a few people focusing on each task rather than the whole group. The full break down of the sub teams and who worked on them can be seen in the table below. In terms of sharing code, we used GitHub to post all code and files so everyone could have access, this also acted as a backup for saving files incase any were lost or damaged.
